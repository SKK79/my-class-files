Reading I — Rules and Safety Guidelines at Central Zoo
12 Marks

Rules and Safety Guidelines at Central Zoo, Jawalakhel, Lalitpur

Please abideabide /əˈbaɪd/ (v.): to accept and follow (rules or decisions) by the rules and safety guidelines.

  • Visitors must stay in line at the ticket counterticket counter (n.): the place where you buy or show your ticket to enter.
  • Visitors are obligedobliged /əˈblaɪdʒd/ (adj.): required to do something by law or rule to show the ticket to the zoo officials when asked.
  • Visitors must not make loud noises. It disturbsdisturbs /dɪˈstɜːbz/ (v.): causes trouble or anxiety to someone the animals and other visitors.
  • Plastic or plastic products are not allowed on the zoo premisespremises /ˈpremɪsɪz/ (n.): the land and buildings of a particular place.
  • Pets like cats or dogs should not be brought inside.
  • Alcohol and smoking are not allowed on the zoo premises. If anyone is found doing so they will be punishedpunished /ˈpʌnɪʃt/ (v.): made to suffer as a result of breaking a rule.
  • No one is allowed to teasetease /tiːz/ (v.): to make fun of or annoy in a playful or unkind way, poke or feed animals.
  • Plucking of buds, flowers and tearing the leaves are punishablepunishable /ˈpʌnɪʃəbl/ (adj.): deserving or able to be punished.
  • The litterlitter /ˈlɪtər/ (n.): rubbish, waste materials left in a public place should be kept in the bins.
  • Selling goods of any type by visitors on the zoo premises is prohibitedprohibited /prəˈhɪbɪtɪd/ (adj.): formally forbidden by a rule or law.

The Zoo is a public place. It is our duty and responsibility to keep it clean and safe. Thank you.

Central Zoo, Jawalakhel, Lalitpur, Nepal

Grammar — should / must / have to / be allowed to

📘 Grammar Notes

ModalUseExample
shouldAdvice / suggestionYou should eat vegetables.
shouldn'tAdvice againstYou shouldn't run here.
mustStrong obligation (speaker's rule)You must wear a uniform.
mustn'tProhibition (not allowed)You mustn't smoke here.
have toExternal obligation (rule/law)You have to show your ticket.
don't have toNo obligation (optional)You don't have to wear a tie.
are allowed toPermission givenYou are allowed to take photos.
aren't allowed toPermission deniedYou aren't allowed to feed animals.

Reading II — Rules at Museum
7 Marks

Amit: Hi Eshika, let's visit the museummuseum /mjuːˈziːəm/ (n.): a building where historical and cultural objects are kept and displayed this Saturday if you are free.

Eshika: Wow! Good idea. But does the museum open on Saturdays?

Amit: Yes, it opens on holidays and remains closed on Tuesday instead.

Eshika: Then, I am ready to go. I am excited to take photos with the antiquesantiques /ænˈtiːks/ (n.): very old and valuable objects in my hand.

Amit: No, no. You are not allowed to touch any items in the museum. You should keep a distance and just observeobserve /əbˈzɜːv/ (v.): to look at carefully them.

Eshika: Oh! So sad. Why is that?

Amit: Those antique items are really rarerare /reər/ (adj.): not common; unusual and valuable ones. If we touch them, they may be ruinedruined /ˈruːɪnd/ (v.): badly damaged or destroyed.

Eshika: Oh, I see. In order to save those items from being ruined, we must keep a distance from them, right?

Amit: Yes! We have to follow certain rules while visiting there.

Eshika: Could you please tell me what we should do and what we should not do there?

Amit: Sure. We have to maintainmaintain /meɪnˈteɪn/ (v.): to keep in the same condition; to preserve silence while visiting. We can take photos of some items that we are allowed to. We should not eat anything there though.

Eshika: Thank you so much, Amit.

Amit: Ok. Then, be ready to go next Saturday.

Eshika: Sure.

Extra Bit — have to / must / should
4 Marks

📘 Key Rules

StructureMeaningExample
mustObligation (my opinion)I must remember to buy a gift.
have toExternal rule (someone says so)You have to look after your wounds.
mustn'tProhibition — no choiceYou mustn't forget your book.
don't have toNo obligation — you chooseYou don't have to wear a tie.
can / are allowed toPermission givenYou can smoke here. = You are allowed to smoke here.
mustn't / not allowed toPermission deniedYou mustn't speak loudly. = You are not allowed to speak loudly.
