Rara Lake

Rara Lake is the biggestbiggest /ˈbɪɡɪst/ (adj. superlative): the largest in size; the superlative form of 'big' lake in Nepal. It is located in Mugu district of Karnali Province. It covers a water surface of 10.8 square kilometres and it is 167 metres deep. It is situated at an altitudealtitude /ˈæltɪtjuːd/ (n.): the height above sea level; how high a place is of 2990 metres with the beautiful surroundings around. It is an important touristtourist /ˈtʊərɪst/ (n.): a person who travels to places for pleasure and sightseeing site of Rara National Park.

There are many amazing features of Rara Lake that the visitors can see and feel. One of the interesting things about Rara Lake is that it is a colourfulcolourful /ˈkʌləfʊl/ (adj.): having many bright and vivid colours; visually striking lake. It exhibitsexhibits /ɪɡˈzɪbɪts/ (v.): shows or displays; makes visible to others three different colours in a day. The reason is still unknown. Similarly, the background of the lake keeps on changing time and again. It is also another mysterymystery /ˈmɪstəri/ (n.): something that is difficult to explain or understand; a puzzle about Rara.

The next amazing feature of Rara Lake is its mirror effectmirror effect /ˈmɪrər ɪˈfekt/ (n.): when the lake water is so clear and still that it perfectly reflects the surroundings like a mirror. The water of the lake is crystal clear. People do not even feel that they are on water while boating. While boating on the lake, the visitors are amazed by the view of the hills and green forests at their front, and exactly the same inside the water with their own images.

Rara Lake is full of magnificentmagnificent /mæɡˈnɪfɪsənt/ (adj.): impressively beautiful and grand; splendid beauty. It is one of the best destinations for the trekkers. Visitors can go hiking on the surrounding hills around the lake. The flora and faunaflora and fauna /ˈflɔːrə ənd ˈfɔːnə/ (n.): the plants (flora) and animals (fauna) found naturally in a particular area are other attractions for the visitors. More than 214 species of birds, 20 species of mammals and 500 types of flowers are found there. The park is the home of many endangeredendangered /ɪnˈdeɪndʒəd/ (adj.): at risk of dying out; very few left in the world wild animals like red panda, musk deer, Himalayan black bear, leopard, and wild boar.

Since Rara is free from dirt and contaminationcontamination /kənˌtæmɪˈneɪʃən/ (n.): the state of being made impure or poisonous by harmful substances, and is less crowded, it is less polluted. The nature lovers can get solitudesolitude /ˈsɒlɪtjuːd/ (n.): the state of being alone and peaceful; silence and quiet on the lap of the Himalayas there. Such a splendid and beautiful Rara Lake can be visited either from the air route by taking the flight to Talcha airport of Mugu from Nepalgunj or through the Karnali Highway by bus or jeep.

Grammar — Comparative & Superlative Adjectives

📌 Grammar Rules: Comparatives & Superlatives

➕ Add -er / -est to one-syllable adjectives: old → older → oldest
➕ If ends in -e, add -r / -st: large → larger → largest
➕ If ends in vowel + consonant, double the consonant: big → bigger → biggest
➕ If ends in consonant + -y, change y→i: happy → happier → happiest
➕ Use more / most for long adjectives: interesting → more interesting → most interesting
⚠️ Irregular: good → better → best  |  bad → worse → worst
