📖Reading I — Festivals of NepalRead carefully

🌸 Buddha Jayanti

Buddha Jayanti is a festival celebrated on the day of Baisakh PurnimaBaisakh Purnima: the full moon day of the Nepali month of Baisakh (April/May). It is the day when Siddhartha Gautam was born, enlightenedenlightened /ɪnˈlaɪtnd/ (v.): gained deep understanding and wisdom; achieved spiritual knowledge, and died. It is also called Buddha Purnima. Buddha was born in 623 BC in Lumbini, Nepal. The festival is celebrated all over Nepal and abroad too to commemoratecommemorate /kəˈmeməreɪt/ (v.): to honour the memory of a person or event his birthday. People go to the monasteriesmonasteries /ˈmɒnəstriz/ (n.): buildings where monks live and worship and stupasstupas /ˈstuːpəz/ (n.): dome-shaped structures built to commemorate Buddha or holy persons to pray to Lord Buddha for peace and prosperity. Special chantingchanting /ˈtʃɑːntɪŋ/ (v.): repeating words or prayers aloud from memory in a musical tone and prayers are performed there on the day.

🕌 Eid

This is the biggest festival of the Muslim community. It is celebrated worldwide on the last day of RamadanRamadan /ˌræməˈdɑːn/ (n.): the ninth month of the Islamic calendar, during which Muslims fast. Ramadan is a month-long fasting. It generally falls in the month of Saun, but the date is confirmed every year according to the Islamic calendar. Muslim people exchange greetings, go to mosquesmosques /mɒsks/ (n.): buildings where Muslims gather to worship, recite NamajNamaj (n.): the formal Islamic prayer, performed five times a day, and pray to Allah for the unity and prosperity of all humankind. They greet and embrace each other with "Eid Mubarak", which means best wishes on the occasion of Eid.

🪔 Tihar (Dipawali)

Tihar is one of the biggest festivals celebrated in Nepal. It is also known as DipawaliDipawali (n.): festival of lights celebrated in Nepal and India; also called Tihar or Diwali. It falls in the month of Kattik. It is celebrated for five days, which is called Yama PanchakYama Panchak: the five days of Tihar, named after Yama, the god of death. The five days are: Kag Tihar (crow), Kukur Tihar (dog), Laxmi Puja, Goru Tihar (cow), and Bhai Tika. People worship goddess LaxmiLaxmi (n.): the Hindu goddess of wealth, fortune and prosperity by lighting lamps and offering PrasadPrasad /prəˈsɑːd/ (n.): food offered to a deity and then distributed to worshippers. On the day of Bhai Tika, sisters offer Tika to their brothers and wish for their longevitylongevity /lɒnˈdʒevɪti/ (n.): long life and prosperity.

☀️ Chhath

Chhath is celebrated on the day of Kattik Shukla Shasthi, immediately after Tihar. People worship goddess Chhathi MaiyaChhathi Maiya: the goddess worshipped during Chhath festival; associated with the sun and pray to the sun (both the rising and the setting sun). It is originatedoriginated /əˈrɪdʒɪneɪtɪd/ (v.): first started or began in a particular place from the Terai region of Nepal, but nowadays it is celebrated in other parts of the country too. People go to the bank of rivers, ponds, or lakes and pray by offering Prasad in the name of Chhathi Maiya.

🎄 Christmas

Christmas is celebrated on the 25th December to commemorate the birth of Jesus Christ. It is celebrated by the Christians in Nepal too. People go to Church and pray to Jesus Christ and exchange greetings and good wishes with each other. They decoratedecorate /ˈdekəreɪt/ (v.): to make something look more attractive by adding things to it their homes, surroundings, and market places with Christmas Trees and lighten the areas. They exchange gifts and have delicious food and enjoy the festival.

📐Grammar — Imperative Sentences8 marks · Auto-scored
💡 Imperative sentences give commands, instructions, or requests. They use the verb without "to" and without "you".
  • Positive: Open the door.   Try your hardest.   Enjoy your work.
  • Negative: Don't shout.   Don't forget your lunch.
  • Polite: Please sit down.   Don't make noise, please.

❓Extra Bit — Question Mark (?) and Comma (,)5 marks
💡 Question Mark (?)
  • At the end of a direct question: Where is the bathroom?
  • After a tag question: You speak English, don't you?
  • After a rhetorical question: Who cares? Are you kidding me?
Comma (,)
  • To separate three or more items: The colours are blue, green and yellow.
  • In a date: He was born on March 17th, 1981.
  • To separate city from state: She was born in Dhading, Bagmati.
  • After introductory words: Yes, I would love to go.
