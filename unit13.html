📄 Text: The Baby Quail

Once upon a time, the Enlightened BeingEnlightened Being /ɪnˈlaɪtnd ˈbiːɪŋ/ (n.): a being of great wisdom and spiritual power (the future Buddha in Jataka tales) was born as a tiny quail. Although he had little feet and wings, he could not yet walk or fly. His parents worked hard for feeding him.

One day, there occurred a big forest fire. All the birds who were able, flew away at the first sign of smoke. As the fire spread, and got closer and closer to the nest of the baby quail, his parents remained with him. Finally, the fire got so close that they too had to fly away to save their lives.

All the trees, big and small, were burning and cracklingcrackling /ˈkrækəlɪŋ/ (v.): making sharp snapping or popping sounds, like burning wood with a loud noise. The little quail saw that everything was being destroyed by the fire. He could do nothing to save himself. At that moment, it seemed that he was helplesshelpless /ˈhelpləs/ (adj.): unable to defend oneself or act without assistance; powerless.

Then, the thoughts came to his mind, "My parents loved me very much. Unselfishlyunselfishly /ʌnˈselfɪʃli/ (adv.): in a way that shows more care for others than for oneself, they built a nest for me, and then fed me without greed. When the fire came, they remained with me until the last moment."

"My parents' kindness was so great. They stayed and risked their lives but still they were helpless to save me. Since they could not carry me, they were forced to fly away alone. Wherever they are, I thank them for loving me so much."

"Now, I am all alone. There is no one I can ask for help. I have wings, but I cannot fly away. I have feet, but I cannot run away. But I can still think. All I have left to use is my mind."

Then, the bird felt an amazing miraclemiracle /ˈmɪrəkl/ (n.): a surprising and welcome event that cannot be explained by natural or scientific laws within his little body. The Enlightened BeingEnlightened Being: the future Buddha — the story is a Jataka tale about the Buddha's past lives within the bird became active and the bird felt powerful. It got the godly power of wish.

So, the little bird wished, "May this land exist with the power of truth. May all birds and other beings, who are still trappedtrapped /træpt/ (adj.): caught in a place and unable to escape by the fire, be saved. And may this spot be safe from fire for a million years!"

And so happened what the bird wished.

Grammar
Connectives: because · since · as · because of
8 marks

📌 Connectives of Reason — Rules

because — used to give a reason (the reason is new or being emphasised):

He was absent because he was sick.

since — used when the reason is already known; focus is on the result:

Since we lost the game, we could not qualify for the semi-final.

as — similar to "since"; often used at the start of a sentence:

As he wasn't ready, we went without him.

because of — used before a noun or noun phrase (NOT a full clause):

Amar is awarded because of his honesty.
ConnectiveFollowed byExample
becausesubject + verb…because it rained
sincesubject + verbSince it rained…
assubject + verbAs it rained…
because ofnoun / noun phrase…because of the rain
