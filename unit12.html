Grammar — Negation

8 marks

📌 Grammar Focus: Making Negative Sentences

We make sentences negative by adding not with the auxiliary verb. Study these patterns:

Present Simple: She spends a lot. → She does not spend a lot.
Present Continuous: They are playing. → They are not playing.
Past Simple: She saw me. → She did not see me.
Present Perfect: I have completed it. → I have not completed it.
Modal: I can finish it. → I cannot finish it.

💡 Contractions: do not = don't | does not = doesn't | did not = didn't | have not = haven't | cannot = can't

Extra Bit — Word Class II

5 marks

📖 Parts of Speech

Noun — names a person, thing, animal, place or idea. Example: Mohan has a pen.

Verb — expresses an action, occurrence, or state. Example: She wrote a letter.

Adjective — describes a noun or pronoun. Example: She is a good girl.

Adverb — modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb. Example: He was badly hurt.
