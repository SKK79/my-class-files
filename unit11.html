12 marks
💡 Before You Read: a) What is the girl doing in the first picture?   b) Have you ever used a mobile phone?

Most of us nowadays carry the world in our pocket! It is amazingamazing /əˈmeɪzɪŋ/ (adj.): causing great surprise or wonder but true. A mobile phone can make us connected to the rest of the world within a second. Today, mothers do not have to remain tense if their sons or daughters are late. Businessmen do not have to queue up before a telephone booth to make an important call. Apart from making calls and sending SMS, the mobile phone is used as a multipurposemultipurpose /ˌmʌltiˈpɜːpəs/ (adj.): having many different uses or functions gadget. It is a calculator, timepiece, calendar, voice recorder, media player, camera, gaming device, net browser and what not.

Though the mobile phone is a giant step of technological advancementtechnological advancement (n.): progress or development in technology, its abuseabuse /əˈbjuːs/ (n.): wrong or harmful use of something cannot at all be overlooked. Talking or texting while driving may lead to accidents. Students misuse this tool in various ways and give way to social pollution. The invention of mobiles phones is a great achievement and plays an important part in our daily life. Its many aspects are beneficial, but some are negative effects as well.

Its development brought convenienceconvenience /kənˈviːniəns/ (n.): the quality of being easy and suitable for your needs and advantages to the world. Communication between people has become quite easy and very fast. We can keep in contact with others from any place on the bus, in a street or in a meeting by sending a message for less than the price of a call. The Internet can be accessed through it and it is also used to click photos and shoot videos.

At the same time, parents can control their children, and it is essentialessential /ɪˈsenʃl/ (adj.): completely necessary; extremely important during emergencies. Business deals can be done on a single call through the cell phone outside the office, in a park or in any shopping center. This invention gets the world closer to a single point. The Internet is also available on the cell phone.

The radiationradiation /ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃn/ (n.): energy sent out as waves or particles, which may be harmful of mobile phones may be dangerous to health and may cause headaches, earaches and blurring vision. These invisibleinvisible /ɪnˈvɪzɪbl/ (adj.): that cannot be seen radiations destroy the cells located in ear and head which can cause damage to the brain and nephrons in the head region. Its use in certain places causes disturbance sometimes, such as in a classroom etc. Mobile phones with cameras are causing privacyprivacy /ˈprɪvəsi/ (n.): the right to keep personal matters secret from others problems such as using it as a hidden camera to take pictures and make videos.

📌 Grammar Rule: Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

Use Present Simple for habits, routines or facts: She reads every morning.

Use Present Continuous for actions happening right now: She is reading right now.

📌 D — Articles: a / an / the

Use a before consonant sounds: a cat, a book

Use an before vowel sounds (a, e, i, o, u): an apple, an orange

Use the for specific nouns (only one, already known, superlatives): the Sun, the Prime Minister, the tallest

No article for proper nouns (rivers with names, planets): The Karnali River — use the for rivers!
