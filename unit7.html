🌍 Human and the Environment

No part of the environment has escaped the effects of pollutionpollution /pəˈluːʃn/ (n.): the presence of harmful substances in the environment. All over the world, the land, the air and the water have been contaminatedcontaminated /kənˈtæmɪneɪtɪd/ (adj.): made impure by contact with harmful substances by an increasing amount of waste materials and substances. The waste comes from factories, homes and vehicles. In some cases, the waste is only an eyesoreeyesore /ˈaɪsɔːr/ (n.): something unpleasant to look at; something that spoils the beauty of surroundings which spoils the beauty of our surroundings. In other cases, it is a serious threatthreat /θret/ (n.): a danger; something likely to cause harm to our health and can result in death.


Some of the most dangerous waste substances are, without doubt, those produced by factories. Industrial waste often contains toxictoxic /ˈtɒksɪk/ (adj.): poisonous; causing harm or death to living things substances that can kill plant and animal life. When there is no proper government control, factories allow these substances to pour into rivers. Their chimneys fill the atmosphere with poisonous fumesfumes /fjuːmz/ (n.): strong, unpleasant, or harmful smoke or gas.


In towns and cities, the use of fossil fuelsfossil fuels: natural fuels like coal, oil and gas formed from remains of living organisms (oil, coal and gas) for heating results in large amounts of carbon dioxidecarbon dioxide /ˌkɑːbən daɪˈɒksaɪd/ (n.): a gas produced by burning fuels, linked to global warming filling the atmosphere. Although some cities have been made smokeless zonessmokeless zones: areas where burning of fuels that produce smoke is not allowed, the exhaust fumes from the vehicles on the roads has meant that air pollution continues to be a major problem.


Each of us is guiltyguilty /ˈɡɪlti/ (adj.): responsible for a specific wrongdoing of polluting our environment to some extent. Almost everything we buy today comes in some sort of container or packaging, made of plastic, glass or aluminiumaluminium /ˌæljʊˈmɪniəm/ (n.): a light, silvery metal used for packaging and cans which we usually throw away. Attempts have been made to encourage factories to recyclerecycle /ˌriːˈsaɪkl/ (v.): to convert waste materials into new materials and objects these materials.


How long can we allow this pollution to continue? Scientists tell us that the increased amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere may soon have disastrousdisastrous /dɪˈzɑːstrəs/ (adj.): causing great damage or suffering effects. They fear that it will raise average temperature around the world and cause the polar ice capspolar ice caps: thick sheets of ice covering the North and South Poles of the Earth to melt. If this happens, the level of the ocean will rise by four to six metres and vast populated areas of the planet will be covered by water.

📝Extra Bit — Kinds of Sentences4 marks
💡 Four kinds of sentences:
  • Declarative (makes a statement): He is the leader of our group. → ends with .
  • Interrogative (asks a question): What's your name?   Do you like apples? → ends with ?
  • Imperative (gives a command): Drink enough water.   Close the door. → ends with .
  • Exclamatory (expresses strong feeling): Wow! What a beautiful garden it is! → ends with !
