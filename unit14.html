How much / How many — Asking about quantities

A. Study: 'How much' vs 'How many'

📏 How much / How many?

How many — used with countable nouns (things you can count: pens, books, students)
👉 How many pens do you have?
How much — used with uncountable nouns (things you can't count: rice, water, money)
👉 How much rice do you eat?

Grammar 8 marks
Quantifiers: many, much, any, no, some, a few, a little, a lot of

A. Study the sentences.

🔢 Quantifiers

many (countable) — I saw many wild animals in Chitwan.
much (uncountable) — There is not much water in the bucket.
any (questions/negatives) — Is there any delicious food?
no (negative emphasis) — I have no time to cook.
a few (small number, countable) — Last night, I read a few pages.
a little (small amount, uncountable) — I drank a little milk.
a lot of / lots of (large quantity, both) — She makes a lot of nice food.
some (positive sentences) — I usually have some toast for breakfast.

📋 Model Report (Rice Items):

The table gives information about the price of rice items. The price of Pokhreli Rice and Jira Masino Rice is equal — NRs 80 per kg. Indian Basmati Rice costs NRs 100 per kg whereas Mansuli Rice costs NRs 65 per kg. Sona Mansuli Rice costs NRs 60 per kg. The price of Taichin Beaten Rice and Normal Beaten Rice is NRs 120 and NRs 70, respectively.

Different items of rice have different prices. Indian Basmati is costlier than Pokhreli and Jira Masino. Sona Mansuli is cheaper than Mansuli. Taichin Beaten Rice has higher price than that of Normal Beaten Rice. Taichin Beaten Rice is the most expensive item and Sona Mansuli is the cheapest one.
